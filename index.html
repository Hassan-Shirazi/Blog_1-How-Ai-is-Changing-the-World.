Key Milestones in AI

1956 - The Birth of AI

The term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined at the Dartmouth Conference, marking the official birth of the field.

1997 - Deep Blue vs. Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue chess computer defeats world champion Garry Kasparov, a landmark moment for machine intelligence.

2011 - Watson on Jeopardy!

IBM's Watson wins the quiz show Jeopardy!, showcasing advances in natural language processing and understanding.

2016 - AlphaGo's Victory

DeepMind's AlphaGo beats Lee Sedol at Go, a game far more complex than chess, using deep reinforcement learning.

2022 - The Generative Explosion

Tools like ChatGPT, DALL-E 2, and Midjourney bring high-powered generative AI to the public, sparking a global revolution.

Beyond Human Limits

As AI's capabilities grow, it will inevitably surpass human performance in many tasks. This presents both incredible opportunities and profound challenges.

Pros: The Upside of Superintelligence

  • Solving complex problems like climate change and disease.
  • Automating dangerous and tedious jobs, freeing humans for creative pursuits.
  • Driving scientific discovery at an unprecedented pace.
  • Creating immense economic abundance and personalized services for all.

Cons: The Risks on the Horizon

  • Job displacement and economic disruption on a massive scale.
  • Ethical dilemmas surrounding autonomous weapons and decision-making.
  • The risk of misuse by malicious actors for surveillance or manipulation.
  • The existential "control problem": ensuring AI remains aligned with human values.
